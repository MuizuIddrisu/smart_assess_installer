PART B - DATA PROTECTION POLICY
Compliance with the Ghana Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843)
and Data Protection (Privacy of Personal Information) Regulations
2016 (L.I. 2227)
================================================================

8. YOU ARE THE DATA CONTROLLER (Act 843, Section 16)
-----------------------------------------------------
As the institution using Smart Assess to process student data, YOU
are the Data Controller under the Ghana Data Protection Act 2012
(Act 843). You are responsible for:

  * Registering with the Data Protection Commission of Ghana
  * Ensuring lawful grounds exist for processing student data
  * Obtaining parental/guardian consent before collecting data
  * Responding to data subject access requests within the statutory
    timeframe (21 days)

9. DATA COLLECTED AND PROCESSED
--------------------------------
Smart Assess collects and processes the following data solely to
perform its academic assessment functions:

  * Student personal data: names, date of birth, gender, class,
    index numbers
  * Academic data: scores, grades, attendance, report cards
  * Staff data: usernames, roles, activity logs (passwords are
    hashed - never stored in plain text)
  * School data: name, type, term/year configuration, grading setup

10. THE EIGHT DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES (Act 843, Section 17)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Smart Assess is designed to support compliance with all eight
principles:

  (a) Accountability   - Your institution is accountable for all
                         data processed through this software.
  (b) Lawfulness       - Data shall only be collected for a
                         specific, lawful, educational purpose.
  (c) Specification    - Data is collected solely for academic
                         assessment and school management.
  (d) Compatibility    - Data shall not be used for purposes
                         incompatible with its collection.
  (e) Quality          - Data shall be accurate and kept current.
  (f) Openness         - Data subjects shall be informed about
                         data collection practices.
  (g) Data Security    - Technical measures protect all data
                         (see Section 11).
  (h) Access           - Data subjects have the right to access
                         their personal data.

11. SECURITY MEASURES
----------------------
Smart Assess implements the following technical controls:

  * All data stored locally in a protected directory
    (%APPDATA%\SmartAssess\) - not on external servers
  * Passwords hashed using industry-standard algorithms
  * Role-based access control limits data to authorised users
  * Session management with automatic timeout
  * Full activity audit log of all data modifications
  * Encrypted HTTPS used for any optional cloud sync

12. DATA STORAGE AND RETENTION
--------------------------------
(a) LOCAL STORAGE: All student and school data is stored exclusively
    on your institution's computer. Smart Assess does NOT transmit
    personal data to external servers without your explicit consent.

(b) RETENTION: You are responsible for setting data retention
    periods in accordance with GES policies and Act 843.
    Uninstalling the software does not automatically delete your
    data unless you choose to do so during uninstallation.

13. RIGHTS OF DATA SUBJECTS (Act 843, Sections 20-28)
-------------------------------------------------------
Students, parents and staff have the right to:
  * Access their personal data held by the institution
  * Correct inaccurate personal data
  * Object to processing in certain circumstances
  * Have their data deleted where no longer necessary

Smart Assess provides tools to export and delete individual student
records to help your institution fulfil these rights.

14. CHILDREN'S DATA (SPECIAL CATEGORY)
----------------------------------------
Smart Assess processes data relating to children (persons under 18).
In accordance with Act 843 and the Children's Act 1998 (Act 560),
institutions must:

  * Obtain parental/guardian consent before collecting student data
  * Use student data solely for educational assessment purposes
  * Not share student data with unauthorised third parties
  * Apply heightened security to protect children's information

15. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION
------------------------------
In the event of a personal data breach, the institution (as Data
Controller) must notify the Data Protection Commission of Ghana
in accordance with Act 843 and take appropriate remedial action.
